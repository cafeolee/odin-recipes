Payares cookies

Description

Payares, as it is called in Asturian, is a small and mountainous village, located on the border with Leon, and is where this traditional recipe comes from.

You can find the original recipe here.

Ingredients

  • 300g clarified butter (500 g normal butter)
  • 200g white sugar
  • 600g wheat flour
  • 1 egg
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Clarify 500 g of butter, which will leave about 300 g, and let it temper.
  2. Mix the butter with the sugar and the pinch of salt until a compact dough is formed.
  3. Add the egg and beat thoroughly.
  4. Gradually add the flour until it becomes a smooth but not sticky dough.
  5. Put a piece of plastic wrap on a smooth surface, spread half of the dough and cover it with another piece of plastic wrap.
  6. Stretch the dough with a kitchen roll to make a 0.5 cm thick sheet. Reserve in the refrigerator for 10 minutes.
  7. Remove plastic wrap and cut the cookies with cutters or with a glass. Bake at 180ÂºC until toasted.
  8. Once toasted, remove from the oven and place them carefully on a wire rack. Sprinkle with plenty of sugar and let them cool. Enjoy!
