Frixuelos

Description

Frixuelos (also known as fayuelos) are a typical recipe from Asturias, a region in northern Spain. It is traditionally eaten during Antroxu (Carnival).

You can find the original recipe here.

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs
  • 200g of flour
  • 500ml of milk
  • Zest of a lemon
  • 1 tablespoon of aniseed liqueur
  • 2 tablespoons of white sugar
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. After beating the eggs add the sugar and continue mixing everything.
  2. Add the aniseed liquer, the milk, the salt and the lemon zest and continue beating.
  3. Gradually add the flour and incorporate it with the rest of the ingredients until you get a homogeneous consistency.
  4. Let it rest for 1 hour and strain the dough to avoid lumps and the remains of the lemon zest.
  5. Dip a brush in a bit of oil and spread it on the frying pan.
  6. Put two tablespoons of the dough and quickly move the pan so it spreads over the entire surface.
  7. After half a minute, try to take off the edges of the frixuelo, if they come off well, put the spoon under the frixuelo and turn it over, leave it another half minute or until it gets golden brown and take it out of the frying pan.
  8. Serve the frixuelos on a plate and sprinkle with sugar. Enjoy!
